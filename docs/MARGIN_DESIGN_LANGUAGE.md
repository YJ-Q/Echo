# Margin Design Language

Date: 2026-07-09

## Core Image

Margin is not a voice chamber and not a dashboard.

Margin is the quiet edge of the page:
- a place where unfinished things can be left
- a place where important traces are kept
- a place where the next line can be continued without pressure

Short version:

> Margin is a paper-and-ink companion space that keeps the live line visible.

## Material Direction

Use these as the primary material signals:
- warm paper
- soft ink
- faint annotations
- lightly layered sheets
- restrained glow under the surface

Avoid these as the dominant direction:
- glossy glass
- metallic hardware
- strong sci-fi light
- dashboard panels
- mascot-like cuteness

## Interface Metaphors

The product should repeatedly feel like:
- a page
- a margin note
- a trace
- an underlined line worth returning to
- a continuation from the previous line

It should not repeatedly feel like:
- a mission control screen
- a queue management tool
- a therapy chart
- a talking smart speaker

## Text Role

Text is the protagonist.

Visual design should support:
- readability
- calm hierarchy
- one live line at a time
- quiet emphasis

Visual design should not support:
- constant interruption
- many equal-priority panels
- pressure-heavy calls to action

## Motion Role

Motion should feel like:
- ink appearing
- paper layers settling
- a note fading in
- a line being gently brought back

Motion should not feel like:
- alerts
- pulses asking for attention
- loud completion celebration
- tech-demo spectacle

## Product Expression

Preferred product language:
- leave this here first
- the line is still here
- continue from the last note
- read this passage softly
- mark the important part

Avoid language like:
- optimize your workflow
- manage your tasks
- analyze your state
- activate voice mode
- boost productivity

## Acceptance Test

The design language is aligned when the user feels:
- I can arrive here before I am organized
- the page is holding something for me
- the important line is still visible
- the interface is guiding me quietly
- I am being accompanied, not managed
